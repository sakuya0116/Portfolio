Virtual Clinical Simulation - VR Clinical Training

arrow_downward line_style image
arrow_back Back to works

Virtual Clinical Simulation (VCS) is a proof of concept prototype of virtual clinical simulation training software. It is aiming to solve the problem for medical students who do not have the accessibility to the real clinical training centre, especially in developing countries.

The prototype is designed around a specific clinical scenario of a young male patient sustaining a motor vehicle crash suffering with hypotensive shock. In the scenario, the student is expected to do a typical patient assessment; asking the patient how they are, monitoring their vitals and conducting a physical assessment. Upon that, discovering that the patient has suffered trauma to his pelvic region.

The protptype is divided into two client ends: VR with Oculus Quest for students and PC for instructors. During the training, students and instructors are able to talk, and the all the changes from both side will automatically synchronize to each other, including vital monitor changes, tools activation and students' positions and actions.​​​​​​​

This project is being published with the paper "Virtual Reality Technology and Remote Digital Application System: An Innovative Approach for Tele-Clinical Simulation and Global Medical Education" on Simulation & Gaming (SEGA) Journal.
